## What the documented frameworks actually support

### Perry Belcher lens

Perry's documented premium teaching supports an item that the market wants even when it is not functionally related to the paid product. His identity-marketing material also supports premiums that help buyers express who they are. Sources: [Perry on premiums](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YZWpW_jsQq4), [Perry on identity marketing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hwNgBrGGMgE), [Perry's funnel guide](https://perrybelcher.com/sales-funnel-template/), and the [DTC Perry Belcher interview](https://www.directtoconsumer.co/podcasts/perry-belcher-on-ecom-tripwires-premiums-and-making-sure-the-cheese-is-worth-the-squeeze).

That supports selecting the trousers as a **wanted-premium candidate** and testing the required demand evidence. It does not prove that this exact perfume buyer wants the trousers, nor does it support saying the products need one another, work together, or cause an attraction outcome.

### Russell Brunson lens

ClickFunnels' official explanations describe one central belief, supporting belief shifts, and a stack that follows the argument. They do not justify a second lower-page retelling of the same story. Sources: [Big Domino, Three Secrets, and Stack](https://www.clickfunnels.com/blog/webinar-marketing-funnel/), [sales-funnel secrets](https://www.clickfunnels.com/blog/sales-funnel-secrets/), and [new-opportunity positioning](https://www.clickfunnels.com/blog/why-funnels-matter-more-than-product/).

The current problem is congruence: the pants are the specific vehicle in the private argument, while Auralo is the item the customer must pay for. A text bridge can clarify roles, but the plugin also needs a private transaction-alignment gate.

### Alex Hormozi lens

Acquisition.com's official material describes value through dream outcome, perceived likelihood, time delay, and effort or sacrifice. Its offer material separately treats offer components, guarantees, bonuses, ethical urgency, fulfilment, and profitability. Sources: [Acquisition.com Offers](https://www.acquisition.com/offers-oo), [Value Equation journal](https://www.acquisition.com/journal-05), the [official $100M Journal PDF](https://www.acquisition.com/hubfs/%24100M%20Journal.pdf?hsLang=en), and the [$100M Offers page](https://shop.acquisition.com/products/100m-offers-hardcover).

The current page makes the FREE item desirable but does not establish the paid item's independent value, perceived likelihood, effort, or verified outcome. That is not a Grand Slam Offer merely because the page looks complete.

### Independent bundle evidence

Usage complementarity can make a recommendation feel like one coherent offer, but category adjacency alone does not prove complementarity. See the [2025 Journal of Retailing paper](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jretai.2025.01.006) and the [large-retailer cross-category field experiment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.00100).

This supports a two-mode plugin contract. It does not support forcing every pair into a combined transformation.

### Cosmetics-claim boundary

For the European market, EU cosmetic claims must be consistent with evidence supporting the claimed effect, including implied claims. See [Commission Regulation (EU) No 655/2013](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2013/655/oj/eng?uri=CELEX%3A32013R0655).

The plugin should therefore treat `pheromone` as part of the supplied product name only. It must not turn that word into an attraction, confidence, behavioural, scientific, ingredient, or performance promise without adequate product-specific evidence.

## Universal two-product relationship model

Use only two top-level modes.

### Mode A: `wanted_premium`

Use when the companion is primarily the action incentive.

Require:

- the paid product has an independent fact-bound reason to buy;
- the companion has current demand or purchase-intent evidence;
- the same buyer plausibly wants both;
- the relationship does not claim shared function;
- `functional_dependency` is false unless an authorized dependency fact exists.

The companion may share an identity, occasion, moment, or nothing beyond buyer desire. The plugin must not invent complementarity.

### Mode B: `complementary_bundle`

Use only when evidence supports joint use, a shared sequence, or a combined outcome.

Require:

- current truth ledgers for both products;
- a distinct role for each item;
- direct joint-use evidence or a bounded two-sided join that matches the same avatar and moment;
- a combined outcome that does not exceed either ledger;
- explicit dependency support before any `needs`, `works with`, or activation language.

If this threshold fails, do not silently fall back to a complementary story. Reassess `wanted_premium`; if premium demand also fails, return `OFFER_MARRIAGE_NOT_EARNED`.

## Ethical impulse-purchase architecture

The plugin can improve impulse conversion by reducing uncertainty and steps. It should not use deception.

### Legitimate levers

- paid/FREE offer understood above the fold;
- relationship understood in the next compact section;
- one consistent CTA phrase;
- visible size and next-step process when technically supported;
- exact shipping, returns, and warranty scope;
- one verified paid-product reason;
- concise objection answers;
- real urgency only;
- no surprise checkout requirements.

### Metrics that matter

Do not optimize only:

- CTA click-through rate;
- drawer opens;
- time on page;
- FREE-item interest.

Optimize:

- completed purchase rate;
- checkout abandonment by step;
- size-stock failure rate;
- refund and exchange rate;
- chargebacks;
- support contacts per order;
- contribution margin after fulfilment and returns;
- paid-product satisfaction;
- companion-product satisfaction.

An impulse purchase that produces high refunds or unprofitable fulfilment is not a successful offer.

- acceptable refund and exchange rates;
